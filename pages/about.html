Past, Present and Future!

Team History - From Humble Beginnings

The NASA Knights team was founded in 1997, and we worked out of a small boiler room with just a handful of students and mentors. Twenty-five years later, we have our own dedicated shop area and meeting space (which was granted to us in 2010) with over thirty dedicated students and mentors.

A Community Team

Our founder, Jeff Seaton, chose New Horizons to host the NASA Knights because of its function as a regional education center--students from school districts all over Southeastern Virginia meet at New Horizons to participate in various programs, from trade school programs to specialized STEM programs. By leveraging this location, Mr. Seaton saw an opportunity to offer FIRST to a large, diverse pool of students in Southeastern Virginia. Because of his vision, every high school student from public, private, or home schooled is welcome to join our team.

Once a NASA Knight, Always a NASA Knight

Our team boasts strong alumni involvement. This year, we have NASA Knights alumni from 2011, 2013, 2015, 2021, and 2022 graduating classes. Two of our most recent sponsorships, Kelvin International and Lockheed Martin, were initiated based on employees' involvement with the NASA Knights as either alumni or mentors.

Impact and Outreach

Local Demostrations

Our team participates in various local demonstrations, bringing in different robots built over the years. In 2023 we demonstrated at several local schools to students of all ages, as well as Christopher Newport University's STEM Day, New Horizons Regional Eduction Center's Center of Autism, and NASA Langley. Our team engages students through various activities and ensures safe and interactive elements during our demonstrations.

Mentoring and Assisting Other FIRST Teams

Recently in the spring of 2022, we helped the FLL Explore Team Legosaurus Rex in preparing for their Explore Festival by hosting a mock judging session. In addition, we provided mentorship to the FLL Challenge Team Circuit Sharks, on a weekly basis during their Fall 2022 season.

Innovative Impact

This summer we attended the Homeschool Educators of Virginia Convention in Richmond to inspire students of all ages to pursue STEM learning where we demonstrated our robot. We also attended the FLL Scrimmage at Christopher Newport University, where our students volunteered as judges, referees, and scorekeepers to keep students engaged in STEM. In addition, we partnered with FRC team 2363, Triple Helix, to build eight FLL tables to help with the local FLL competitions for the coming years.

Inclusion

This year, with new female mentors and students, we organized a series to facilitate women-specific conversations in STEM. We started female-centric safe spaces for students to discuss challenges in STEM fields. In November 2022, we cohosted the off-season competition Rumble in the Roads at Menchville High School along with teams 2363 and 1610.
